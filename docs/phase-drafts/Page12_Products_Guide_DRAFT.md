## Overview

This page translates the evidence reviewed on *Skincare Ingredients*, *Nutrition & Supplements*, and *Device Therapy* into a curated, specific product list — organized by category, with the active ingredient(s) that matter, an approximate current price range, and a budget/premium alternative pairing. Products are included because their **active ingredients have meaningful evidence support** (per the relevant evidence page), not because of brand popularity, marketing spend, or influencer presence.

> **Quick Takeaway**
> - **Evidence quality and price are not reliably correlated.** Several of the best-evidenced actives (retinol, niacinamide, vitamin C) are available in well-formulated products under $30; some premium products' main differentiator is texture/luxury feel rather than meaningfully different active-ingredient efficacy.
> - **This page will go stale faster than any other page on the site.** Specific products, prices, and formulations change; the underlying *active ingredient* evidence (covered on Skincare Ingredients) is far more durable than any specific product recommendation.
> - **Device categories (LED masks, RF tools) sit in a meaningfully higher price tier** than topicals — worth weighing against the evidence-tier and cost-effectiveness ratings on *Device Therapy* before purchasing.

## Common Misconceptions

> **Myth vs Fact**
> **Myth:** A higher price reliably indicates a more effective or higher-quality product.
> **Fact:** For several of the best-evidenced actives on this page (retinol, niacinamide, vitamin C), budget and premium formulations at comparable concentrations show similar evidence-based efficacy. Premium pricing often reflects texture, packaging, fragrance, or brand positioning rather than a meaningfully different active-ingredient outcome.

---

## Research Gaps

This page's "gap" is structural rather than evidentiary: **product-specific independent testing is rare.** Most of the evidence reviewed throughout this site is for active ingredients at studied concentrations, not for specific commercial formulations — a product containing an evidence-backed active at an appropriate concentration is reasonably assumed to perform similarly to the studied formulation, but head-to-head independent testing between specific commercial products is uncommon in the literature.

---

## Clinical Bottom Line

Price and evidence-based efficacy are not reliably correlated across the categories on this page — several of the strongest-evidenced actives (sunscreen, retinol, niacinamide, vitamin C) are available in well-formulated, inexpensive products, while some premium pricing reflects texture and brand positioning rather than superior active-ingredient outcomes. The clearest exception is collagen supplements, where the underlying funding-bias finding (see *Skincare Ingredients*) means this page can't confidently recommend a premium option as "worth the extra cost" the way it can for other categories. LED devices represent the steepest price jump on this page and should be weighed against the *Cost vs Benefit Calculator* before purchasing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is The Ordinary actually as good as more expensive brands?**
For several specific actives at comparable concentrations (retinol, niacinamide), yes — the active ingredient evidence doesn't distinguish by brand. Formulation extras (additional supporting ingredients, texture, packaging) vary, but the core active's evidence-based mechanism doesn't change by price tier.

**Should I buy an LED mask or spend that money elsewhere?**
See the *Cost vs Benefit Calculator* for a direct comparison — LED therapy has real independent RCT evidence (per *Device Therapy*), but at a meaningfully higher price point than topicals with comparable or stronger evidence tiers (sunscreen, retinoids).

**Why doesn't this page recommend specific "best" products for every category in the original brief (e.g., exhaustive options)?**
Per project direction, this page is a curated list (5-10 per category) rather than an exhaustive searchable database, prioritizing products with clear active-ingredient evidence support over comprehensive brand coverage.

**How often are the prices on this page updated?**
Prices reflect current market research at the time of writing and will drift — treat specific dollar figures as directional (budget/mid/premium tier) rather than exact, and expect this page to need more frequent revisiting than most others on the site.
